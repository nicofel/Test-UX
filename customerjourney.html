THE CASE


We continued using the mock-up company Elbolaget AB. They wanted to see how their customers interacted with their website so that they could see if they had to make improvements and make their website easier for users. We chose Maja as she is a student with a tight budget and wants to keep track of her electricity usage so she can use her money on more fun things.




WHO IS THE CUSTOMER JOURNEY BASED ON?


The customer journey we created was based on the persona of Maja Persson, 24, who is a law student at Uppsala University. She's keen to make sure she has enough money since she is a student and wants to avoid unnecessary costs. Maja currently lives in a 40-square-meter student apartment with her partner, with who she often consults when making important decisions.

Life as a student is hectic, so there is no time to focus on electricity consumption or contracts. Maja would rather spend time on all the fun activities student life offers. To quickly get an overview of her costs, Maja prefers to use her mobile phone and finds it frustrating if there are too many steps in the process. High electricity prices are one thing that worries Maja in her everyday life.

INTERVIEW


We found positive and negative aspects that arose during the user's interaction with Elbolaget AB through a qualitative interview and user tests.

The interview was conducted remotely based on prepared questions with the possibility of follow-up questions and in-depth discussions. During the interview, we found out that Maja's goal was to get a better overview of her finances; hence her main priority was the cost of electricity contracts.

She had been in touch with customer service on a few occasions when there had been a power cut to find out how long it would take for the power to come back. She said that the customer service employee was very accommodating and friendly.

During the interview, it became clear that Maja had no previous knowledge of Elbolaget AB and first came in contact with the company through the Elskling tool, which she used to find, compare, and sign a new contract when she first moved into her apartment.

TESTING


Maja thought it was a bit tedious that there was not a ready-made app for Elbolaget AB, she felt that it would improve the experience of the service. The answers from the interview gave a good understanding of why the user chose Elbolaget AB.

Based on the interview, we were able to find several touchpoints where the user interacted with the company. The first customer journey is mainly based on interview data.

We found out how the user became a customer, why she applied to Elbolaget AB, and how she felt. Maja had to share her screen and show how she maneuvered through the website.

During the test, she was also asked to continuously tell us how she was thinking when using the service, and whether it was easy or challenging to achieve the goal.

All tests were conducted on mobile as this is what Maja used the most. We decided to test the mobile version because Maja is a person who uses her phone a lot and always has it with her. So we felt that a good mobile version was important to focus on.

WHY ARE CUSTOMER JOURNEYS IMPORTANT?


It is a strategic approach to understanding customer expectations and is important to optimize the customer experience. It is as essential for a small and medium-sized company as for a larger company. Mapping out the journey has a lot of benefits like:

  • Benchmarking the customer experience desired by the customers against what they actually receive.

  • Understanding the differences in buyer personas as they move from prospect to conversion through the buying funnel.

  • Creating a logical order to your buyer journey

The most significant benefit is simply understanding the customers more. The better you know their expectations, the more you tailor the customer experience to their needs.


WHAT DID I LEARN?


I got some more insight on how to conduct an interview with general questions and more in-depth questions. I also got to observe a user test where the persona had to try out the website.

I also learned how to write a customer journey and how important they are. It is a good tool to see how the customers experience a website and to see if it needs improvements.
